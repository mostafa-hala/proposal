01.

Executive Summary

This proposal introduces a next-generation underwater security system designed to extend SLG’s capabilities into subsea environments. The solution, engineered by QueNeu, centers on an intelligent Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) equipped with advanced sensing technologies capable of detecting gas leaks, oil contamination, and underwater threats in real time.

By integrating cutting-edge materials such as Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs), Quantum Dots, and Topological Materials, the system transforms traditional reactive inspection into proactive, intelligent underwater security operations.

02.

Problem Statement

Critical infrastructure such as ports, pipelines, and offshore facilities face increasing underwater risks that often go undetected by conventional means. Traditional systems rely primarily on visual inspection (cameras), which is limited and often reactive.

🫧

Subsurface Gas Leaks

Often invisible until they reach a critical mass or surface, causing catastrophic failure before detection.

🛢️

Hydrocarbon Leaks

Difficult to differentiate from natural background noise in murky water without expensive laboratory delays.

🛡️

Asymmetric Threats

Unknown objects or unauthorized equipment placed near sensitive assets remain hidden from optical sensors.

I. GAS DETECTION (MOF + Chamber)

Goal: Real-time detection of Methane ($CH_4$) or Hydrogen Sulfide ($H_2S$) from subsea pipelines.

Core Sensors

  • 🔹 MQ-4: Methane Optimized
  • 🔹 MQ-136: H2S Optimized

MOF Integration

  • 🔹 ZIF-8: Zinc-based for CH-4
  • 🔹 Cu-BTC: HKUST-1 for H2S

Technical Components:

Membrane:

PTFE (Teflon) or PDMS. Prevents water ingress while allowing gas molecules to pass.

Pump:

Mini diaphragm pump ($5V/12V$) for active air sampling within the chamber.

Chamber:

Sealed Acrylic/Aluminum housing with controlled airflow mounts.

Operational Logic:
1

ROV nears pipeline ➔ Leaked gas dissolves in water ➔ Hits membrane.

2

Gas molecules permeate PTFE ➔ Enter sealed air-filled chamber.

3

MOF (ZIF-8) captures molecules ➔ Local concentration increases significantly.

4

MQ-4 sensor detects change ➔ Arduino/Pixhawk triggers surface alarm.

The Core Mechanism:
  • • UV LED fires pulse into the water (365nm).
  • • Oil particles absorb UV and emit a weak glow (Fluorescence).
  • • Quantum Dots match the oil's glow frequency, amplifying it locally.
  • • Photodiode measures the surge in light intensity and alerts the operator.
